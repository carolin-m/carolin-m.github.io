## Current Position <br>
________________
I am currently working in the field of **theoretical physical chemistry** and **machine learning** in the group of **Prof. Alexandre Tkatchenko** at the [University of Luxemburg](https://www.tcpunilu.com/). I investigate the mechanism of (light-induced) isomerization reactions by means of computational chemistry and machine learning methods. My research is dedicated to the development and application of systematic hierarchy
of efficient machine learning based methods for predicting the changes in vibrational and electronic absorption spectra upon (photo-)isomerization.

> • Computational Chemistry • Machine Learning • Molecular Dynamics • Isomerization • <br />

## Research Interests and Keywords <br>
________________
{: style="text-align: justify" }
With my previous experience in the field of spectroscopy and data science, many of my research projects are based on a combination of **spectroscopic** (*e.g.*, resonance Ramann, (transient) absorption and (time-resolved) emission spectroscopy, and spectro-electrochemistry), **computational chemistry** (*e.g.*, TD-DFT predictions of state specific ground and excited state properties) and **cheminformatics** methods (*e.g.*, SVD, MCR and global/target lifetime analysis).

**Spectroscopy** <br />

**Computational Chemistry**  <br />

**Cheminformatics** <br />
